About Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture (MoTAC)

The Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture in Ghana is dedicated to fostering sustainable tourism development. With a focus on cultural preservation and socio-economic benefits, MoTAC drives initiatives that transform communities and enrich the visitor experience.

Skills Development through Tourism

  • Informing Ideas: Encouraging innovative tourism concepts.
  • Problem Solving: Addressing challenges like infrastructure and service quality.
  • Technical Skills: Training in hospitality, event management, and digital marketing.
  • Professional Skills: Enhancing customer service, management, and leadership.
  • Communication Skills: Improving interactions with diverse tourist demographics.

Highlighted Tourist Sites

Kakum National Park

Located in the Central Region, this park is renowned for its canopy walkway, offering breathtaking views and supporting local conservation efforts.

Kwahu Easter Paragliding Festival

A cultural extravaganza where paragliding enthusiasts soar above the scenic Kwahu mountains, celebrating Easter with community joy and adventure.

Cape Coast Castle

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this castle tells the somber history of the slave trade while also highlighting Ghana's rich coastal heritage.

Larabanga Mosque

One of West Africa's oldest mosques, known as the "Mecca of West Africa", offering a glimpse into the Islamic heritage of Ghana.

Mole National Park

Ghana's largest wildlife refuge, where visitors can engage in safari tours to see elephants, antelopes, and a variety of other animals in their natural habitat.
