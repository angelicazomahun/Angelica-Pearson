About Us

Green Ghana Tours began as a passionate vision to showcase the vibrant culture, lush landscapes, and rich history of Ghana to travelers worldwide. Launched in early 2023, the company aimed to offer sustainable, eco-friendly travel experiences that celebrate Ghana’s natural beauty while supporting local communities. The website, which we conceptualized together at that time, was designed to be the digital heartbeat of this mission. The Initial Creation When we first "created" the Green Ghana Tours website, the goal was simplicity and authenticity. The homepage featured a stunning aerial view of Independent square, , paired with a tagline: “Explore The Rich Culture Heritage OF Ghana.” The site included key sections: Tours: Highlighting trips to the Greater Accra Region. Sustainability: A page detailing the company's commitment to eco-tourism, like planting a tree for every traveler. and the strong relationship with our clients MOTAC. Now, in February 2025, Green Ghana Tours has grown. Based on "previous feedback", we’ve added more depth to the website. Inspired by Ghana’s heritage, this includes virtual previews of activities—like learning to drum with the Ga people or cooking jollof rice with a local family. Looking Ahead Green Ghana Tours’ website is now a living platform, evolving with the company’s mission to make Ghana a top eco-tourism destination. Future plans might include a virtual reality tour teaser or partnerships with global green travel networks—ideas we could explore together next!

Client background

The Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture has its roots in the colonial era, when the British colonial administration established the Department of Archaeology and Anthropology in 1946 [1]. After Ghana gained independence in 1957, the department was renamed the Department of Cultural Affairs and transferred to the Ministry of Education [1].
In 1970, the Department of Cultural Affairs was upgraded to a full-fledged ministry, known as the Ministry of Culture [1]. In 1993, the ministry was renamed the Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture, with a broader mandate to promote Ghana's tourism industry, arts, and culture [1].
Some key milestones in the history of MoTAC include:
1. Establishment of the Ghana Tourist Board: In 1973, the Ghana Tourist Board was established to promote Ghana's tourism industry [2].
2. Launch of the Ghana Cultural Policy: In 2004, MoTAC launched the Ghana Cultural Policy, which aimed to promote Ghana's cultural heritage and develop the creative industries [3].
3. Hosting of international events: MoTAC has hosted several international events, including the 2006 African Cup of Nations and the 2010 African Union Summit

Our Mission And Vission

Mission

- "To provide accurate and up-to-date information on tourist attractions, activities, and experiences in Ghana, promoting tourism and cultural exchange."
- "To showcase the beauty, diversity, and richness of Ghana's culture, history, and natural heritage, inspiring visitors to explore and discover our country
- "To provide comprehensive, accurate, and up-to-date information on Ghana's tourist attractions, activities, and experiences, empowering visitors to make informed travel decisions."
- "To promote sustainable tourism practices, supporting local communities, preserving cultural heritage, and protecting the environment."
- "To foster collaboration and partnerships among stakeholders in Ghana's tourism industry, driving growth, innovation, and excellence."

Vission

- "To establish Ghana as a premier tourist destination, renowned for its warm hospitality, rich cultural heritage, and breathtaking natural beauty."
- "To create a digital platform that showcases the best of Ghana, inspiring visitors to explore, discover, and fall in love with our country."
- "To become the go-to online resource for travelers, tour operators, and travel agents seeking information on Ghana's tourist attractions, activities, and experiences."
- "To become the leading online platform for promoting tourism in Ghana, driving economic growth and development through sustainable tourism practices."
- "To create a vibrant online community that celebrates Ghana's unique cultural identity, fosters global connections, and inspires a love of travel and exploration."
